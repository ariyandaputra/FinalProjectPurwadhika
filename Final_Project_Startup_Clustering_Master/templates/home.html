Startup Clustering

Using Crunchbase Data from 2000 until 2013

by: Muhamad Ariyanda Putra

Goals :

This clustering is made to be a benchmark for other Startup, wether they are new or old. By benchmarking your startup to other startup around the world you can compare your funding and strive to be better.

Clustering is made by using:

  • How many times a startup is funded
  • What is the type of funding the startup uses
  • How much the total funding of a startup
  • Where is the startup head office located

Dataset and Clustering Process:

  • 25,080 unique startup around the world
  • Each unique startup funding and funding type is collected
  • There are 7 unique funding type that used for consideration
  • There are 123 unique startup head office country location
  • Data is process using normalization process using MinMaxScaler to reduce the range and the weight because of it
  • Data is then reduced to 2 component using Principal Component Analysis to avoid multicollinearity and nicer scatterplot

Cluster Short Description

Angel Startup

  • Took several funding mostly on Angel and some venture funding
  • Second lowest fund count
  • The lowest in term of average and maximum fund

Big Startup

  • Mostly took series A and Venture funding
  • A minimum of 2 times funding and the highest average fund count
  • The highest in term of minimum funding and average funding

Venture Startup

  • Mostly took funding in form of Venture funding
  • Below average fund count
  • Above average in term of average total fund and has the highest maximum total fund

Early to Mid Startup

  • Majority took Series A and Series B
  • Above average fund count
  • Below average in average total fund

Late Fund Startup

  • Took funding mostly on Series B, Series C, or other funding
  • Has the lowest average of fund count
  • Has the second highest average total fund

Small Startup

  • Took funding mostly on Series A or Angel fund
  • 2nd highest fund count
  • 2nd lowest average total fund
